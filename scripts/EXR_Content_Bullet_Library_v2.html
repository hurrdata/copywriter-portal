EXR Content Bullet Library

Strategic content framework for Extra Space Storage location pages — designed for brand consistency and data-informed storytelling

Overview

Every Extra Space Storage location page features an "About Our Location" section with exactly 4 content bullets. Each bullet is selected from a curated library of 12 distinct types, organized across 5 categories. This ensures that every location's story is grounded in what's actually true about its surroundings and who shops there — not templated copy.

Core Principle: The 4 bullets are selected based on confirmed location data and customer draw patterns unique to each facility. This transforms generic "convenient location" claims into specific, verifiable assertions that resonate with readers searching for storage solutions.

The framework accommodates four distinct location types, each with different audience priorities and selection logic. Whether customers are drawn by neighborhood familiarity, mixed-draw patterns, metro-wide accessibility, or a major anchor, the bullet library adapts to tell the right story.

Location Types & Audiences

Understanding the location type shapes which bullets matter most. Here's how to categorize any Extra Space Storage facility:

Neighborhood Locations
Customers are overwhelmingly local. The store's appeal is rooted in being the convenient choice for people in the immediate area. Hyperlocal specificity matters most — readers want to know if their neighborhood, subdivision, or school zone is represented.
Mixed-Draw Locations
A strong local base, but meaningful traffic from adjacent communities or a specific nearby anchor (university, military base, etc.). These locations benefit from both hyperlocal grounding and acknowledgment of the secondary draw source.
Metro/Wide-Draw Locations
Customers come from a broad geography, often pulled by a major anchor — airport, stadium, medical center, or urban business district. Geographic orientation and access become more important than neighborhood callouts; readers are less likely to recognize subdivision names.
Anchor-Driven Locations
The store's draw pattern is explained primarily by proximity to one dominant attractor. The anchor leads the story; community context takes a secondary role. Strong for locations where a military base, major university, or airport is the primary driver of demand.

Category 1: Core Location Identity

These bullets establish geographic context and the store's relationship to its community. Every location needs at least one from this group; many will feature two or three.

Home Community
Core Identity

Anchors the page to the store's primary service area. Sets geographic context and tells readers exactly where this location is and who it serves. Always present in the 4-bullet set.

Example Copy
"Located in the heart of Downtown Seattle, our storage facility serves the core urban community with convenient climate-controlled units just minutes from Pike Place Market and the business district."
Icon Guidance
Simple location pin or crosshair marker in brand green. Must be immediately recognizable as a geographic indicator.
Nearby Neighborhoods
Core Identity

Names specific neighborhoods, subdivisions, and residential communities near the store. Highest value for neighborhood locations — readers in those areas instantly recognize the names and feel the location is made for them.

Example Copy
"Serving the Heights, Craftsman Hills, and Riverside neighborhoods, our storage facility has been the trusted choice for families and professionals throughout the North Shore community for over a decade."
Icon Guidance
Row of stylized residential buildings or houses. Emphasizes residential community. Avoid generic grid patterns — aim for a warm, neighborhood-focused visual.
Second City Draw
Core Identity

Signals when a meaningful share of customers come from a different municipality than the store's home city. Tells readers in that second city: "this location serves you too." Critical for wide-draw and anchor-driven locations where cross-border traffic is significant.

Example Copy
"While based in Springdale, our location is equally convenient for residents of neighboring Millbrook, just 2 miles away. Many of our customers are from both communities."
Icon Guidance
Two location pins or markers connected by an arrow or bridge. Visually communicates cross-community connectivity without suggesting travel distance.

Category 2: Geographic Access

Transform vague "convenient location" claims into specific, verifiable statements about highway access and transportation proximity.

Interstate/Highway Exit
Geographic Access

Confirms distance and exit number for the nearest major interstate or highway. Replaces vague "convenient freeway access" with a measurable, specific claim that helps customers verify they can reach the facility quickly.

Example Copy
"Just 1/2 mile from Exit 47 on I-70, our facility offers quick access for anyone commuting through the valley or traveling along the corridor."
Icon Guidance
Interstate shield or highway marker in brand green. Should feel official and directional. Consider subtle road markings or arrow elements.
Airport Proximity
Geographic Access

Highlights proximity to a commercial airport. Strong appeal for business travelers, airport employees, and airport-corridor businesses. Customers benefit from short drive times before/after flights and temporary storage during travel.

Example Copy
"Just 8 minutes from Denver International Airport, we're the preferred storage choice for business travelers, airline employees, and anyone needing secure storage while traveling."
Icon Guidance
Airplane in departure or takeoff pose, or departure/arrival signage style. Should feel travel-oriented and professional.

Category 3: Community Anchors

Named institutions and landmarks that create predictable storage demand and help readers understand the area's character and who lives nearby.

University/College Proximity
Community Anchors

Near a four-year university or community college, with confirmed distance and institution name. Speaks directly to student housing needs, semester-break storage, and young professional demand. Signals an educated, dynamic community.

Example Copy
"Less than a mile from Ohio State's main campus, our facility is the go-to choice for students needing summer storage solutions and recent graduates building their lives in Columbus."
Icon Guidance
Graduation cap, academic building, or mortarboard symbol. Should feel educational and aspirational without being cartoonish.
Military Base/Community
Community Anchors

Near an active military installation. Speaks to PCS (Permanent Change of Station) moves, deployment storage, on/off-base living transitions, and veteran-owned business support. A strong signal of stable, family-oriented community demand.

Example Copy
"Close to Fort Bragg and serving military families throughout the installation and surrounding areas, we understand the unique storage needs of active duty personnel and PCS moves."
Icon Guidance
Military star, dog tags, or service insignia. Respectful, dignified, and immediately recognizable. Avoid overly aggressive imagery.
Local Schools
Community Anchors

Near one or more named K–12 schools. Signals a family-embedded, community-rooted location. Copy emphasizes family life logistics — downsizing as kids grow, seasonal items, hobby storage — not academic performance.

Example Copy
"Nestled in the heart of the Lincoln school district, near Lincoln Elementary and Lincoln High, our facility serves the families and professionals who call this neighborhood home."
Icon Guidance
Schoolhouse, pencil, backpack, or apple. Warm and welcoming, emphasizing community rather than academics. Should feel family-friendly.
Notable Nearby Landmark
Community Anchors

A highly recognizable destination near the store — hospital, professional sports stadium, historic landmark, or major attraction. Used as a geographic orientation anchor to help readers instantly understand the location's place in a familiar landscape.

Example Copy
"Just a short drive from Fenway Park and the Museum of Fine Arts, our location is perfectly positioned for arts patrons, sports fans, and professionals working in the Back Bay area."
Icon Guidance
Gold or accent-colored star or landmark icon. Suggests prestige, visibility, and significance without being branded.

Category 4: Lifestyle & Recreation

When the store's amenities support these claims, lifestyle bullets create authentic alignment with nearby recreation and outdoor communities.

Marina/Waterfront
Lifestyle & Recreation

Near a marina, boat ramp, or waterfront access. Paired with boat/watercraft storage capability when available. Signals alignment with boating, fishing, water sports communities, and seasonal waterfront enthusiasts.

Example Copy
"Minutes from Marina Bay and accessible to the waterfront, our facility is home to local boaters, fishing enthusiasts, and sailors who need secure storage for equipment and seasonal gear."
Icon Guidance
Sailboat, anchor, or wave motif. Should feel nautical and inviting without being overly decorative. Keep lines clean and recognizable.
RV Park/Outdoor Recreation
Lifestyle & Recreation

Near an RV park or campground. Paired with RV/oversized vehicle parking when available. Speaks to outdoor enthusiasts, weekend adventurers, and travelers who need secure storage between trips without the year-round RV lot expense.

Example Copy
"Close to the Ponderosa RV Park and surrounded by hiking and camping opportunities, our facility welcomes outdoor enthusiasts who need seasonal storage for RVs, boats, and adventure gear."
Icon Guidance
RV silhouette, tent with trees, or campfire icon. Emphasize adventure and outdoor lifestyle. Should feel welcoming and active.

Category 5: Urban Residential

In dense urban markets where residential subdivisions don't exist, this category names specific multifamily communities, loft buildings, and condo complexes to establish hyperlocal relevance.

Urban Residential Communities
Urban Residential

Names specific apartment complexes, condo buildings, and loft communities near the store. This is the urban equivalent of the "Nearby Neighborhoods" bullet — it gives readers in those buildings immediate recognition and signals the location was made for them.

Example Copy
"Serving residents of the Metropolitan Lofts, Riverside Towers, and the Grandview Residences, our facility is the trusted storage choice for urban professionals and growing families throughout the downtown core."
Icon Guidance
Stylized apartment or loft building with visible windows. Should feel modern, urban, and residential. Avoid generic or overly corporate imagery.

Design & Editorial Principles

Persona Shapes Tone, Not Selection
The same university bullet reads very differently depending on whether the location skews toward students and young professionals or toward academic professionals and families. Selection is data-driven; tone is persona-driven. A family-focused location might say "close to excellent schools," while a young professional area might say "steps from campus life." Both are true; both serve their audience.
Copy Must Be Verifiable
Every neighborhood name, distance, exit number, and institution claim is confirmed against location data before the bullet copy is finalized. This isn't aspirational copy — it's fact-based content grounded in geography and customer behavior. Vague claims like "near shopping and dining" have no place here.
Exactly 4 Bullets, Always
The constraint of 4 bullets forces prioritization and discipline. It prevents "everything but the kitchen sink" lists and ensures each bullet earns its place on the page. This discipline strengthens the overall narrative.
Embrace Specificity
Name the neighborhoods, schools, universities, and landmarks by their actual names. Readers recognize "Riverside Lofts" and "Lincoln High School" in ways they never will recognize "nearby residential communities" or "local schools." Specificity builds trust and relevance.
Icons Are Guides, Not Rigid Rules
The icon guidance provided here is directional. UX and brand teams should adapt icons to match Extra Space Storage's visual identity while preserving the semantic meaning and green accent color (#3D9E30).
Strip Away Technical Details
The audience is the client's brand and UX teams, not internal developers or data engineers. No mention of APIs, data sources, methodology, or pipeline references. The focus is storytelling, not mechanics.
